Tourist Attractions

Necropolis
Glasgow

The Glasgow Necropolis is a Victorian cemetery in Glasgow, Scotland. It is on a low but very prominent hill to the east of Glasgow Cathedral (St. Mungo's Cathedral). Fifty thousand individuals have been buried here. Typical for the period, only a small percentage are named on monuments and not every grave has a stone. Approximately 3,500 monuments exist here.

Riverside Museum
Glasgow

The Riverside Museum (formerly known as the Glasgow Museum of Transport) is a museum in Glasgow, designed by Zaha Hadid Architects, housed in a building at Pointhouse Quay in the Glasgow Harbour regeneration district of Glasgow, Scotland. The building opened in June 2011, winning the 2013 European Museum of the Year Award.

University of Glasgow
Glasgow

Established in 1451, the University of Glasgow is ranked amongst the world's top 100 universities. The university is an illustrious member of the Russell Group of Institutions. The university is recognized for its world-leading research expertise in the history of art, veterinary medicine, cancer studies, accounting, and finance.

More details...
Kelvingrove Gallery of Art and Museum
Glasgow

Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum is one of Scotland's most popular free attractions, featuring 22 themed, state-of-the-art galleries displaying an astonishing 8000 objects. The collections at Kelvingrove are extensive, wide-ranging and internationally significant. They include natural history, arms and armour, art from many art movements, historical periods, and much more.

More details...
Science Centre
Glasgow

Glasgow Science Centre is one of Scotland's must-see visitor attractions. It has lots of activities to keep visitors of all ages entertained for hours. There are two acres of interactive exhibits, workshops, shows, activities, a planetarium and an IMAX cinema. The Science Show Theatre is the place for live science shows full of whizzes, bangs, pops and flashes.

Travel Links

There are many ways you can travel around Glasgow but here are our favourite ways to get around!


Bike - If you wish to travel around Glasgow on a bike you can do by using OVO Bikes. Visit the OVO Bikes website to find out how it works.

Subway - The subway is one of the easiest ways to get around the city centre, west end and southside of Glasgow. Running every 4 minutes at peak times, it takes just 24 minutes to complete a circuit of the 15 stations. Visit the Strathclyde Partnership for Transport (SPT) website for more information.

Train - Glasgow Central Station links Glasgow by rail to the south, with Glasgow Queen Street Station operating routes mainly to Edinburgh and the north. Visit the ScotRail website for information on all routes and timetables.

Bus - First Bus Glasgow operates over 80 routes across the city and has a variety of tickets to choose from, including day tickets allowing unlimited travel in your selected area all day, or a weekly ticket if you’re staying here longer. Click Here for more information.

Sightseeing bus - The City Sightseeing Glasgow open-top bus tour is one of the most convenient ways to visit Glasgow’s top tourist attractions and iconic sights. Tickets are available from the City Sightseeing Glasgow website.
